## What this paper has

**Strongest contribution.** The §5.2 → §6.4 *prediction-then-verification* chain — *single-layer attention ablation is null on 6/6 mechanism panel; therefore signal is multi-layer-redundant; therefore single-direction mitigation will fail across datasets where the relevant layer migrates; verified by LEACE rank-1 backfiring +56 % on ChartQA after Tally-only calibration*. This is a falsifiable mechanism-grounded prediction with empirical follow-through, not a post-hoc rationalization. Combined with the (a − m) paired-inpaint contrast (§6.2.1) — same scene, digit pixel removed, generic distraction auto-subtracted — it is the load-bearing methodological structure of the paper. This pair survived four rounds of stress-testing with hedges added but no claim retracted on N > 1 evidence. If any single piece of this paper is cited in 5 years, it will be this chain.

**Citable in 5 years.** "*Choi et al. 2026 showed that VLM cross-modal anchor signal is multi-layer redundant, predicting and verifying single-direction mitigation failure.*" That is the headline a future activation-steering paper will cite to motivate going multi-direction in their own setting. It is also the headline that requires the *least hedging* to remain accurate at the 5-year mark — even if cross-architecture E6 generalization fails, the prediction-verification structure on the LLaVA-OneVision panel stands.

The b-arm em +8.8 pp result is more *surprising* but more *fragile* — until R4 CRIT-3 alternatives (random K=8 / non-anchor calibration) are run, a 5-year-later citation could read it as either "calibration contrast captures anchor-specific representational direction" or "K/d ≈ 0.002 mild regularization." That ambiguity is what disqualifies it from the citable-finding spot today.

## What this paper is missing

### THE sharpest question (the bar-raiser ask)

**Does the (a − m) K=8 subspace amplitude grow over Thinking-mode trace generation, and does that growth quantitatively predict the ×12.7 correct-base amplification?**

The paper currently has three parallel threads that *should* be one but are not interlocked:

1. **§4.6 (behavioral):** Qwen3-VL-Thinking amplifies anchor pull ×1.6 in adopt, ×2.9 in df, ×12.7 in the correct-base subset df ratio. Reported as a finding.
2. **§5.2 (mechanism, different model panel):** anchor signal is multi-layer redundant in the residual stream; single-direction methods predicted to fail.
3. **§6.2 (mitigation, OneVision):** the K=8 subspace at L=26 captures the anchor representational signature *and* (per §6.3) some wrong-base error mode.

Read together, the obvious *unasked* question is: **if the K=8 subspace is the representational signature of the anchor effect, then in a model where the anchor effect is amplified ×12.7 (Thinking), the K=8 amplitude (or the analogous calibrated subspace on Qwen3-VL) should be *measurably larger* — and that amplitude should be observable as the trace generates token by token.** This is exactly the kind of analytical pass that turns three weak claims into one strong mechanism-grounded claim.

Concretely, one of two experiments — both feasible in days, not weeks — closes this:

- **Cheap form (recommended).** Reuse the existing OneVision K=8 V_K[L=26] subspace as a measuring instrument. On Qwen3-VL-Instruct vs Qwen3-VL-Thinking generations of the MathVista γ-β set, capture the residual stream at the cross-architecture-aligned layer (or, since Qwen3-VL has a different layer count, the proportional layer). Project onto V_K and report the squared norm ‖V_K^T h(x_t)‖² as the trace position t advances. If Thinking shows a *monotonically growing amplitude* that Instruct does not — and if the growth correlates with the per-question shift toward the anchor — *the paper has interlocked §4.6 with §6.2 mechanically*. The (×12.7) becomes a *measurable amplitude growth in residual space*, not just a behavioral ratio.
- **Cleaner form (preferred if compute permits).** Calibrate Qwen3-VL-Instruct's own (a − m) subspace at its own peak layer (a few H100-hours), then track its amplitude over Thinking traces. Avoids the cross-architecture transfer assumption. This is the version that also doubles as a *partial* CRIT-1 cross-architecture replication.

If the experiment lands positive, this paper has *one* paragraph that simultaneously: (a) elevates §4.6 from N=1 existence proof to a mechanism claim with residual-stream-level evidence, (b) demonstrates the K=8 subspace is not a one-model artifact but transfers as a *measurement instrument* (a softer cross-architecture claim than re-tuning), (c) closes the §5/§6 ↔ §4.6 narrative gap that currently makes the paper read as three findings rather than one chain, and (d) creates the bridge to the LRM-as-amplifier literature [Wang 2025a] that §1.5 (6) currently asserts but does not deliver mechanistically.

If it lands negative, §4.6 honestly retreats to "behavioral existence proof, mechanism unidentified" — which is still publishable but properly fenced. *Either way, the paper resolves an ambiguity it currently lets sit*.

This is the single experiment that, if added, would tip my judgment from "Solid Findings, top of band" to "Main, lower half of accepted." Without it, the paper has three good things that do not amplify each other.

## What I would NOT change

The bar-raiser's protective duty: list things the paper does well that would be tempting to "polish" but are correct as-is.

- **The (a − m) paired-inpaint contrast design rationale (§6.2.1 Insight).** R4 explicitly acknowledged this as un-attackable. It is the single cleanest methodological move in the paper, and the (b, m, d) → (a − m) traceability from behavioral §4.3 to mitigation §6.2 is genuine mechanism-to-mitigation linkage, not post-hoc justification. **Protect it; do not "improve" it.**
- **The R4 hedge propagation across six callsites for CRIT-1 (single-model E6).** Six callsites with identical scope qualifier is the *correct* response to the N=1 critique short of retitling. Do not over-hedge further; the paper is honest now without being defeatist.
- **The §6.2.3 paired-sids "5/5 부호 음, 4/5 noise floor 상회, InfoVQA inconclusive" reframing.** This is exactly the right register — point-estimate consistency reported with the InfoVQA inconclusive fence explicit. Bonferroni-20 acknowledged as deferred. Do not water this further; the headline is now defensible.
- **The strict free-lunch criterion's Δem(non-anchored) ≥ 0 clause.** R4 MIN-8 framed this as "celebration criterion"; R4 Rebuttal 1 correctly defended it as a *load-bearing screening rule* that killed every baseline in Table 7. The criterion is doing work, not decorating. If a future reviewer pushes back, neutralize the *name* ("4-clause Pareto+") not the *substance*.
- **The 7-axis hedge stack on §1.5 (1) "first-evidence" framing.** Independent + rendered-digit + open-numeric-estimation, with VLMBias/Wang 2025b/FigStep differentiators all explicit in §2. R3 made this load-bearing and R3 response delivered. Do not add more qualifiers.
- **The §5.3 honest disclosure of OneVision dataset-dependent peak (L=14 vs L=27).** This is the kind of self-undermining transparency that builds reviewer trust. The paper acknowledges within-OneVision peak migration *before* claiming the (L=26, K=8, α=1.0) cell generalizes across datasets — and the generalization holds despite the disclosure. That sequencing is rhetorically and methodologically correct. Do not move §5.3 later.
- **The §4.7 InternVL3 chart-stack reversal as a documented boundary case.** Two candidate mechanisms (B2/B3) tested and falsified, paper does not paper over. This is the negative-results discipline a Main-tier paper needs and a Findings-tier paper benefits from. Keep.
