## Summary

Three of the paper's six contribution claims survive a hostile-reviewer prior-work check; three are overstated relative to the literature and one descriptive sentence in §2 is **factually inaccurate** about VLMBias. (i) The "first VLM cross-modal numerical anchoring evaluation" framing in §1.5 (1) is defensible *with stacked-qualifier hedging*, but Round 2's softening of (6) to "first-evidence" was not propagated to (1) — the asymmetric claim register is a reviewer trap. (ii) The (a − m) paired-contrast → SVD subspace projection in §6.2 is a *novel application* of an existing paradigm (CAA-style paired contrastive activation steering, Panickssery 2023), not a novel technique class — the paper does not currently cite CAA, ITI, or the LEACE original paper in Related Work, which is the largest single missing-prior-work gap. (iii) The "strict free-lunch" criterion is a real conceptual contribution (Δ_anchor < 0 ∧ Δ_em(a) ≥ 0 ∧ Δ_em(b) ≥ 0 ∧ Δ_held-out ≥ 0 simultaneously) but the *naming* is borderline — closest precedent "No Free Lunch in LM Bias Mitigation" [arXiv:2511.18635] uses the exact "free lunch" idiom for a related claim (negative result), so the paper should defend its term against being read as a quibble with that paper. (iv) §2's description of VLMBias as using "*의미 클래스 라벨*" (semantic class labels) and measuring "*반사실 카운팅 실패*" (counterfactual counting failure) is **factually wrong** — VLMBias uses *actual visual objects* (Adidas logos, animals, chess) as stimuli and measures counting accuracy on *familiar subjects*, no class-label cue. The mischaracterization makes the §2 differentiator load-bearing for novelty look weaker, not stronger.

Venue: at the **borderline between Main and Findings**, leaning Main *if* (a) the missing CAA/ITI/LEACE prior-work block is added and §2 VLMBias description is corrected, and (b) §1.5 (1) is softened to match (6)'s "first-evidence" register. As shipped on 2026-05-09, the novelty axis would attract one or two senior reviewers' challenges that the authors cannot dismiss.

## Strengths

- **Strict free-lunch criterion is a genuine conceptual contribution.** The four-clause conjunction (Δdf < 0 on anchoring task ∧ Δem(a) ≥ 0 on anchored arm ∧ Δem(b) ≥ 0 on non-anchored arm ∧ Δ ≥ −1 pp on held-out capability benchmarks) is *operationally* a Pareto-improvement criterion, but the *specific Δem(b) ≥ 0 clause* — that mitigation must improve exact-match on the no-anchor baseline arm — is the load-bearing novelty. No prior debiasing paper I could locate adopts that clause as a screening criterion before adopting an intervention. The closest precedents (LEACE concept scrubbing, CAA hallucination steering) demonstrate "minimally reduces capabilities" rhetorically but do not formalize a four-clause acceptance bar. Axis E passes.

- **The (a − m) calibration contrast design choice is a genuine methodological move, even if the activation-steering family is not novel.** §6.2.1 Insight is correct: SVD on (h_a − h_m) where m is digit-pixel-inpainted is *the* clean isolation of digit-pixel signal, automatically subtracting both generic distraction (the "second image" effect) and scene-background variance. CAA's contrastive pair (positive vs negative behavioral examples) does not have a visual-modality analog of "same scene, digit pixel removed", and the typographic-attack literature (Wang et al. 2025b, FigStep) does not use paired-contrast calibration at all. The (a − m) construct is a non-trivial application-specific design.

- **First-evidence VLM result on reasoning-amplifies-anchoring (γ-β).** Wang et al. 2025a [arXiv:2504.09946] is verified text-only — abstract makes no mention of VLMs or multimodal experiments. The paper's §4.6 / §1.5 (6) "first VLM result" claim, after Round 2's softening to "first-evidence" with N=1 caveat, is defensible. Axis G passes.

- **The four-archetype encoder-family ordering claim (§4.4 Insight 3, §5.1 Insight 1) is genuinely novel positioning.** "Encoder의 typographic robustness가 LM backbone 능력보다 anchor robustness를 더 결정한다" is a falsifiable cross-cut between the typographic-attack literature (Goh 2021, Wang 2025b NAACL, Hufe 2025 Dyslexify) and the VLM bias literature (VLMBias, AIpsych, CIVET). I found no prior paper that explicitly ties anchor pull strength to encoder family rather than LM backbone. This sub-claim is a load-bearing differentiator the paper does not currently emphasize enough in §1.5.

- **§5.2 multi-layer redundancy → §6.4 single-direction failure prediction-then-verify chain.** This is the paper's strongest mechanism-to-mitigation argument, and it is genuinely novel as a concrete prediction. The chain (single-layer null → single-direction will fail → empirically verified +56 % LEACE backfire on ChartQA) is the kind of falsifiable prediction that distinguishes Main-tier mechanism papers from behavioral-only papers (per project.md §"What EMNLP Main demands"). Round 1's CIT-3 +57 % ActAdd traceability gap was correctly fixed in Round 1; the Round-3 challenge is whether the *baseline coverage* is broad enough — see W3 below.

## Weaknesses (ordered by severity)

- **[CRIT-N1]** **§2 VLMBias description is factually inaccurate.** Paper says: *"VLMBias [Vo, Nguyen et al., 2025]는 가장 포괄적인 VLM cognitive bias benchmark이지만 단서가 *의미 클래스 라벨*이며 *반사실 카운팅 실패*를 측정한다."* I WebFetched the arXiv abstract (verbatim): *"we test how the knowledge about popular subjects hurt the accuracy of vision language models (VLMs) on standard, objective visual tasks of counting and identification. We find that state-of-the-art VLMs are strongly biased (e.g., unable to recognize the 4th stripe has been added to a 3-stripe Adidas logo) scoring an average of 17.05% accuracy in counting (e.g., counting stripes in an Adidas-like logo) across 7 diverse domains from animals, logos, chess, board games, optical illusions, to patterned grids. Removing image backgrounds nearly doubles accuracy (21.09 percentage points), revealing that contextual visual cues trigger these biased responses."* The cue is **the actual logo / animal / chess image itself** (memorized prior knowledge about the subject), NOT a "semantic class label" (i.e., a text label like "Adidas" shown to the model). And the measurement IS counterfactual counting in a sense, but the mischaracterization "의미 클래스 라벨" overstates the dissimilarity. Two consequences: (a) the differentiator becomes weaker than the paper claims (both papers use *visual cues that trigger biased numerical answers*; the difference is "memorized-subject background image" vs "anchor digit image", which is a finer distinction than "label" vs "rendered digit"); (b) any reviewer who has read VLMBias will catch this immediately. **Required:** rewrite §2 VLMBias paragraph with the actual stimuli description, and reframe the differentiator on *measurement target* (open-ended numerical estimation with arbitrary anchor draw vs counting accuracy on familiar subjects with known correct answer) and *anchor source* (independent rendered digit unrelated to question vs subject of the question itself), not on "label vs digit".

- **[CRIT-N2]** **§2 has no prior-work coverage of activation-steering / representation-engineering / multi-direction subspace methods, despite the paper's §6.2/§6.4 differentiator claims rest on out-performing exactly this class.** The Related Work section §2 currently splits into (i) LLM anchoring lineage (Tversky / Mussweiler / Jones / Echterhoff / Wang 2025a / Huang 2025), (ii) Multimodal cognitive bias (VLMBias / AIpsych / CIVET / Tinted Frames), (iii) Typographic attack + mechanism (Goh 2021 / Wang 2025b / FigStep / Weng 2024). **Missing entirely:** the residual-stream-intervention literature that §6.4 / §6.5 directly compare against. The paper invokes ActAdd, LEACE, CAA-style direction methods in §6 as *comparison baselines* — but never cites them as Related Work in §2. The most consequential missing citations:
  - **CAA — Contrastive Activation Addition [Panickssery et al., arXiv:2312.06681, ACL 2024].** This is the paper's nearest methodological neighbor: "computes 'steering vectors' by averaging the difference in residual stream activations between pairs of positive and negative examples" — the paper's (a − m) contrast is *exactly this paradigm*, just SVD'd to K=8 dim instead of mean-pooled to rank-1. The paper claims novelty for "multi-direction subspace projection over single-direction methods"; CAA is the canonical single-direction precedent and *must* be cited as the predecessor that the multi-direction extension is generalizing.
  - **ITI — Inference-Time Intervention [Li et al., arXiv:2306.03341, NeurIPS 2023 spotlight].** Multi-direction (multiple attention heads), residual-stream, calibrated on ~few hundred examples, label-free at inference — i.e., everything the paper claims as novel about its E6 method, *except* operating on attention-head outputs rather than residual stream. ITI is the closest deployable precedent.
  - **LEACE — Belrose et al. [arXiv:2306.03819, NeurIPS 2023].** Cited in §6.4/§6.5 as "rank-1 closed-form" but **missing from References list and §2**. Notice: LEACE is *not* inherently rank-1 (the original paper does general affine concept erasure of any rank); the paper's framing "single-direction LEACE rank-1 closed-form" is one specific instantiation. §6.4 prose currently reads as if "LEACE = rank-1" is the standard, which over-states the prior method's limitation. Either cite LEACE properly with the multi-rank capability acknowledged (and explain what rank-1 setting was actually run), or rephrase to "we ran LEACE in its rank-1 default setting on ChartQA gt ∈ [0,8] and observed +56 % backfire (`E6-tally-only-rerun-tracker.md:480`)".
  
  These three missing baselines are *exactly* the prior-work class the paper claims to outperform; without them in §2, the §6.5 Table 7 comparison reads as cherry-picked. **Required:** add a §2 paragraph "Activation steering and concept erasure" covering CAA / ITI / LEACE (and ideally the Hufe 2025 Dyslexify mechanistic-defense paper, already in references but only one mention in §2), and explicitly position E6 as "multi-direction extension of CAA-style paired-contrast steering, applied at residual stream rather than attention heads (ITI)".

- **[CRIT-N3]** **§1.5 (1) "VLM 최초의 cross-modal numerical anchoring 평가" was not softened in Round 2 to match (6).** Round 2 correctly softened (6) from "VLM에 일반화됨을 입증" to "first-evidence VLM 결과 (단일 architecture pair, cross-architecture 일반화는 §8.2 한계)". But (1) still reads "VLM 최초의" — strict claim, no hedge. The §1.2 Round-2 paragraph stacked-qualifier defense — *"독립 rendered-digit 이미지를 cross-modal anchor로 open numeric VQA에 전달한 사례는 없다"* — relies on three qualifiers (*independent* / *rendered-digit* / *open numeric VQA*) any one of which a hostile reviewer can challenge:
  - "Independent" (vs target-image-overlaid) — distinguishes from typographic attack (Wang 2025b NAACL, FigStep). Defensible.
  - "Rendered-digit" (vs class label) — claimed to distinguish from VLMBias, but per [CRIT-N1] above, VLMBias does NOT use class labels; it uses memorized-subject visual content. The "rendered-digit vs class-label" distinction is ill-founded.
  - "Open numeric VQA" (vs counting / classification) — distinguishes from VLMBias (counting accuracy) and typographic attacks (classification flip). Defensible.
  
  Two of three qualifiers are defensible (independent + open numeric VQA); one (rendered-digit vs class-label) collapses on the [CRIT-N1] correction. The "first VLM" claim survives if reframed as "first VLM evaluation of open-ended numerical anchoring with an *independent* anchor image", but a hostile reviewer will still ask: "Does VLMBias's logo-counting-with-prior-knowledge meet your criterion if I substitute 'memorized prior digit count' for 'rendered digit'? The mechanism is structurally identical — visual content biasing a numerical answer." The paper needs to *anticipate* this question. **Required:** soften §1.5 (1) to "VLM 최초의 *independent-anchor* cross-modal numerical anchoring 평가" or "VLM 최초의 cross-modal *open-numeric-estimation* anchoring 평가 (independent rendered-digit anchor; counterfactual-counting setups in [VLMBias, 2505.23941] are a complementary class with shared mechanism question)". Match (6)'s register.

- **[MAJOR-N4]** **The "strict free-lunch" criterion needs a defense paragraph against being read as a Pareto-improvement renaming.** Per E-axis search, the term "strict free-lunch" does not appear in the bias-mitigation literature in this exact form. The closest precedent is *"No Free Lunch in Language Model Bias Mitigation"* [arXiv:2511.18635, Nov 2025] — which uses the same idiom but for a *negative* result (targeted bias reduction collateral damage in 31.5% of untargeted dimensions). The paper's "strict free-lunch" must therefore (a) define the criterion formally (the four clauses listed in Strengths above), (b) cite arXiv:2511.18635 as a prior negative result the paper's E6 method *contradicts*, and (c) defend why "strict" is meaningful — i.e., what does the *strict* prefix add over plain "free lunch" / "Pareto improvement"? Currently §6.2.3 / abstract / §8.1 just use the term without defining it. **Required:** add a one-sentence formal definition somewhere in §6.2.3 ("*strict free-lunch*: Δdf(anchoring) < 0 AND Δem(anchored) ≥ 0 AND Δem(non-anchored) ≥ 0 AND Δ(held-out capability macro) ≥ 0 simultaneously"), and a sentence positioning against arXiv:2511.18635.

- **[MAJOR-N5]** **§2 framing of Weng et al. 2024 EMNLP Main is structurally unfair to that paper and load-bearing for novelty.** The paper §2 lists Weng as a Main-tier mitigation comparator for "going one step further". I WebFetched: Weng et al. studies *gender bias* (image-feature contribution to gendered answers in MSCOCO/PASCAL-SENTENCE) with *image encoder blurring* mitigation (22.03 % / 9.04 % bias reduction). It's not numerical anchoring, not residual-stream steering, not deployable in the same sense, and the "22 % mitigation" headline is on a different metric (bias reduction in caption gender associations, not direction-follow). The current §2 implicitly reads "Weng got into Main with N% mitigation; we go further with K%" — but the comparison class is wrong. The paper's actual EMNLP-Main precedent should be argued either as (i) "we provide the *same kind* of mechanism→mitigation chain Weng provided, applied to anchoring instead of gender bias", or (ii) "the Main bar Weng cleared is mechanism-grounded mitigation; we clear it with more rigorous capability preservation". Currently §2 implies a tighter analogy than is fair. **Required:** rewrite the Weng paragraph to be precise about what bias / mechanism / mitigation type is being compared, and either drop the implicit "we go one step further" framing or specify the dimension on which the paper goes further (paired-contrast subspace, capability preservation, strict-free-lunch).

- **[MAJOR-N6]** **Lou & Sun [arXiv:2412.06593] is in the References list but never described in §2.** This is the only other 2024 paper specifically titled "Anchoring Bias in [Large Language Models]: An Experimental Study" — a direct semantic neighbor on the LLM side. It tests CoT, Thoughts of Principles, Ignoring Anchor Hints, and Reflection as mitigations and reports they're *insufficient*. Currently the paper §2 lists Jones-Steinhardt and Echterhoff as the LLM-anchoring precursors and skips Lou-Sun. Either (a) describe Lou-Sun as "LLM anchoring confirmed; simple prompt-level mitigations insufficient — motivates our representation-level approach in VLMs" and add to §2 LLM-anchoring lineage paragraph, or (b) drop from References. As a citation orphan, it weakens the paper's literature awareness. **Required:** add one sentence to §2 LLM-anchoring lineage.

- **[MAJOR-N7]** **§1.5 contributions list does not surface the "encoder-family-determines-archetype" finding (§4.4 Insight 3 / §5.1 Insight 1) as a contribution, even though it is one of the most genuinely novel sub-results.** Current §1.5 (4) is "6-model 메커니즘 panel single-layer ablation null + multi-layer redundancy 분석" — generic. The actual mechanism contribution is two-fold: (a) single-layer null → multi-direction prediction (already in (5)), and (b) *encoder family, not LM backbone, determines anchor-attention archetype* (4 archetypes, ConvNeXt/CLIP/InternViT mid-stack cluster despite different LM backbones). (b) is a falsifiable cross-cut against the typographic-attack literature [Goh 2021, Hufe 2025 Dyslexify] and is genuinely novel. Currently §1.5 hides it. **Should-fix:** split (4) into (4a) single-layer null + multi-layer redundancy, (4b) encoder-family-determines-archetype with 4-archetype taxonomy. This also addresses Round-2 §1.5 rhythm concern more cleanly — three small (4a/4b/4c) sentences are easier to read than one compound (4).

- **[MINOR-N8]** **§2's "*다음을 측정하지 않는다*" (none of them measure cross-modal numerical anchoring) sentence is too strong.** The actual fact is: none of the cited (VLMBias / AIpsych / CIVET / Tinted Frames) measure *numerical anchoring with an independent rendered-digit anchor image on open-ended numeric VQA*. AIpsych [Liu 2025, arXiv:2507.03123] does cover some psychology-grounded VLM biases that may overlap on the *uncertainty modulation* axis — the paper has not actually verified that AIpsych's "consistency" sub-bias has no overlap with anchor pull modulation. This is a stylistic-strength issue, not a factual one. **Should-fix:** soften "이들 중 어느 것도 cross-modal numerical anchoring을 측정하지 않는다" to "이들 중 어느 것도 *독립 rendered-digit anchor 이미지에 대한 open-numeric-estimation*을 측정하지 않는다" (matches the (1) softening proposed in CRIT-N3).

- **[MINOR-N9]** **§1.5 (5) "strict free-lunch까지 동시에 검증한다" — capability preservation framing is correct, but the term "strict free-lunch" appears in (5) without forward-link to where it's defined.** Reader sees the term in §1.5 (5), doesn't know what *strict* means, has to wait until §6.2.3 / §6.5 to learn. **Should-fix:** "strict free-lunch (anchoring task 효과 + 양 arm em 상승 + 일반 능력 보존 동시 충족; §6.5에서 정의)" inline.

- **[MINOR-N10]** **§2 paragraph 3 ("Typographic attack과 mechanism 연구") could mention Hufe et al. 2025 Dyslexify in *prose*, not just refs.** Dyslexify is an arXiv 2508.20570 mechanistic-defense paper; the paper has it in References list but the §2 prose mentions only Goh / Wang 2025b / FigStep / Weng. Dyslexify is the closest *defensive* mechanism paper to the paper's E6, and the encoder-family-decides-typographic-robustness finding (§4.4 Insight 3) is exactly what Dyslexify addresses on the CLIP side. **Should-fix:** add one sentence: "Dyslexify [Hufe et al., 2025] mechanistically defends CLIP against typographic attacks at the encoder side; this paper's E6 acts at the residual stream of the language model, complementary."

### E. Strict free-lunch criterion novelty

- **Conceptual novelty assessment:** The four-clause conjunction (Δdf < 0 ∧ Δem(a) ≥ 0 ∧ Δem(b) ≥ 0 ∧ Δ_held-out ≥ 0) is more demanding than standard "Pareto improvement" because it explicitly screens on *non-anchored arm* exact-match — i.e., the criterion requires that mitigation must not only not hurt non-anchored cases but *improve them on average* (Δem(b) ≥ 0). No prior debiasing paper I located adopts that clause. The paper's own §6.3 Insight 1 honestly disambiguates "anchor mitigation" claim from "general debiasing" — this is the kind of conceptual care that distinguishes the strict-free-lunch criterion from a renaming.

- **Defensibility against Pareto-improvement reframing:** Acceptable, but the *current draft does not articulate the criterion formally*. As written, "strict free-lunch" reads as a colloquial summary of "didn't break held-out benchmarks", which any reasonable reader would call Pareto improvement. The **Round-3 fix** is to add ~3 sentences in §6.5 (or in §6.2.3) defining the criterion: 4 clauses, why each is load-bearing, why "strict" ≠ Pareto. Should also cite arXiv:2511.18635 as a direct precedent on which the strict-free-lunch criterion is in friction (Δem(b) ≥ 0 specifically defends against the cross-category collateral damage that 2511.18635 documents).

- **Verdict (axis E):** ✅ *holds* with the formal-definition fix; ⚠ *currently weakly defended* without it.
